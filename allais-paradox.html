What does this mean?

The Allais Paradox shows that real human choices violate Expected Utility Theory in a systematic way. We tend to overweight certainty — getting $1M for sure feels psychologically special, beyond what its probability justifies. This is called the certainty effect, and it's one of the foundations of behavioral economics and prospect theory. Choosing 1A+2B isn't "irrational" in the everyday sense — it's just inconsistent with the mathematical axioms of EU theory.
